I have so many things I want to say. Mostly, I want to say them to an attentive, receptive audience. (Don’t we all?) But I don’t exactly know how to find that right now, so I’m going to just put my thoughts out into the ether and see what happens. I long to speak kindness, empathy, and beauty with my writing. May God guide my hand to do this!

I want this blog to be like me: a mix of many things. I’ll probably share some of my photos, I’ll talk about art and literature, and I probably can’t keep myself from talking about politics, but mainly I want to share about how mental illness affects my life and my faith. I have been diagnosed with many things over the years, but most recently with Bipolar Disorder and Generalized Anxiety Disorder. My hope is that I will find strength in sharing my experiences, and let others know that they are not alone. I will strive always for authenticity, but sometimes I will fail. Please hold me accountable to this, but do it with gentleness! I’m learning along with you here, and trying things that scare me, and that I may not be awesome at. But maybe I’ll surprise us all!

So, faith. I believe in the only, sovereign, ever-present God of the Bible. Yup, that God. He is complicated, but He is also beautiful and so, so, so BIG. Before you cringe and run away, please hear me out. It is possible to miss truth when it’s right in front of you because of the filter that you take all information in through. I know this from experience. For all my life, I’ve filtered Scripture and almost everything anyone has ever said to me through the belief that “I am not enough.” When you start with that assumption, even the best well-intentioned advice or encouragement to grow becomes an exposure of your inadequacy. God can then not love me while simultaneously grieving my sin because all He can see (from my perspective) is the way my failures prove that I’m not enough to be worthy of His, or anyone’s, love.

See how that skews everything? Hang with me, there is good news at the end.

But first, I want to acknowledge how the Church (all Christians around the world) and especially the American church, has missed the point many times. We have truly made God in our own image, and not let Him be His glorious Self. We have made Him into the ultimate self-help guru, who came to make us more powerful, healthy, wealthy, and wise. Because we have married Biblical truth with an obsession with progress, many people have been left in the dust (the differently abled, the chronically ill, those with nontraditional sexuality, and people of color, just to name a few). And those not left in the dust often feel compelled to hide behind a mask of smiles, lest they be exposed as the one person who isn’t getting better. God becomes a cosmic scorekeeper, a judgmental parent with hands on hips, tapping His foot in frustration with our slow progress.

We have denied our corporate sins as a church, because we can’t admit that we’re not making progress all the time. So I have witnessed God people deny racism, sexism, and discrimination against the differently abled and mentally ill. It breaks my heart, and I do not condone or excuse it. God offers free forgiveness (purchased at great cost by Jesus; see below), if we’d just admit how horribly we have messed up and that we can’t undo the damage we’ve done without Divine intervention!

If you really look at the life of Jesus, it is hard to claim that He made any progress in His life. He went from being a simple carpenter to being an itinerant preacher to losing almost all his followers and dying in pain and humiliation. Where’s the progress in that??

But in His life I’ve found incredibly beautiful truth. He knows pain. He knows failure. He knows poverty. That means He knows what I’ve been through. He gets it. He didn’t consider Himself above it. He was willing to go through all of that to show all of us how much He wants to be with us. Always. No matter how hard, no matter how messy our lives are. Even when we are making no progress. We might even be getting worse. When Jesus hung on that cross, rejected, humiliated and abandoned, what could have been worse than that? And yet He did it; He demonstrated how evil and destructive sin really is, and in doing so validated how excruciating it is when others hurt us. He gets that. He suffered, because someone had to take responsibility for all the evil in the world. To acknowledge how horrific it really was, and to show what He was willing to go through to give us His eternal, unconditional acceptance. Think about that! Unconditional acceptance?! Who does that???

Jesus doesn’t just love you, He LIKES you!

And this is what has saved my life. It has helped me believe that I actually am enough; and that I’m not failing at life or at being human. And that experiencing the world differently from most other people is not a bad thing, and actually reflects the creativity and complexity of God.

I will try to flesh this out more as I go. Right now, I just had to get something down. I have to start somewhere.

I look forward to walking this path with you. Let’s see where it takes us.
